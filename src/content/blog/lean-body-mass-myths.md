## Myth 1: Muscle weighs more than fat

A kilogram of each weighs exactly one kilogram. Muscle is simply **denser**, so it takes less space. That's why two people at the same weight can look completely different — the full explanation is in [muscle vs fat explained](/guides/muscle-vs-fat-explained).

## Myth 2: BMI tells you enough

BMI can't separate muscle from fat, so it mislabels muscular people as overweight. [Lean body mass vs BMI](/tools/lean-body-mass-vs-bmi) covers why composition wins.

## Myth 3: You can't build muscle while losing fat

Beginners and people returning to training often do both at once. The key is enough protein and resistance work — see [lean body mass for weight loss](/tools/lean-body-mass-for-weight-loss).

## Myth 4: Cardio is the best way to change your body

Cardio helps your heart and burns calories, but resistance training is what preserves and builds lean mass during a diet.

## Myth 5: Lean mass loss with age is unavoidable

Some decline is natural, but resistance training slows it dramatically. Estimate where you stand today on the [LBM calculator](/tools/lean-body-mass-calculator).
